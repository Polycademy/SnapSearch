* What is a Usage?

Each request to the SnapSearch API either results in content being dynamically scraped using the SnapSearch scrapers, or content being fetched from the cache. A usage refers to a request that does not hit the cache, and initiates a fresh snapshot.

The number of usages per month is used for the calculation of the cost per month. The number of requests per month is not capped, but the number of usages per month can be capped in your control panel.

If you’ve exceeded your usage cap, our middleware simply returns your content normally. So it’s best to keep your cap above average in case of search engine traffic spikes.

Cost Estimator

Use this tool to estimate your monthly payment. If you’re using a 24 hr cache lifetime, requests per month are roughly cut in half when converted to usages per month. The cost per month is calculated from total usages minus free usage cap, multiplied by the price per usage, rounded to the nearest cent.

This is an estimation, to get proper usage figures, we recommend that you try our service with the free usage cap, and use our analytics to determine how many usages per month your web application needs.
